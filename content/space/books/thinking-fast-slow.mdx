The book that made me realize how much of what I thought was "rational" reasoning was actually System 1 pattern-matching dressed up in post-hoc justification. Directly influenced how I think about building AI systems that assist human decision-making — if the humans using the system are subject to anchoring, availability bias, and loss aversion, the system needs to be designed with that in mind, not against it.

The chapter on the planning fallacy alone is worth the read for any engineer who has ever estimated a project timeline.
