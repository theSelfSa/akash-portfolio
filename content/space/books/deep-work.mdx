Cal Newport's argument is simple but uncomfortable: the ability to focus without distraction on cognitively demanding work is becoming increasingly rare and increasingly valuable. Most knowledge workers — including most engineers — are optimizing for the appearance of productivity rather than the output of it.

What I took from this: the most important work I do (designing systems, writing complex code, understanding a new ML paper) requires uninterrupted blocks of 2–4 hours. Protecting those blocks is not a luxury, it is the job.
